Monitoring the number of beachcast seabirds and marine mammals can help with the detection of mortality events triggered by natural perturbations and human causes such as harmful algal blooms and oil spills.

Trend: beachcast seabirds

BeachCOMBER monitoring program
BeachCOMBERS in CeNCOOS data catalog

Encounter rate (birds/km) of carcasses of five commonly encountered species on mainland beach segments in the Southern California Bight by coastal ocean mammal and bird education and research surveys (BeachCOMBERS) program volunteers.

Click for Details The mean and trend of the last five years is evaluated. The dotted line box indicates the evaluation period and upper and lower standard deviation of the full time series. Annual data for Cassin’s auklet and northern fulmar are calculated through February of the following year. For more information, consult BeachCombers_CCIEA_Strandings_South in the CINMS 2016 Condition Report.
